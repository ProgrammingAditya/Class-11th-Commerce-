INTRODUCTION TO
ACCOUNTING

Meaning · Objectives · Importance

Bhai, pehle ye samajh ki duniya ka har bada business — chahe wo Reliance ho ya tere ghar ke paas wali parchoon ki dukan — sab ek hi system pe tikke hain: Accounting. Agar hisaab sahi nahi, toh business khatam.

Topic 1 — Accounting Asliyat mein hai kya?

Accounting sirf ek register nahi hai — ye ek 7-step ka process hai jo machine ki tarah kaam karta hai:
1
Identification (Pehchaano)

Sabse pehle hum ye dekhte hain ki kaunsa kaam "Financial" hai. Agar manager ne employee ko daanta — wo accounting mein nahi aayega. Par agar manager ne salary di — wo turant record hoga.

2
Measurement (Naapo)

Har transaction ko "Rupees" mein convert karna zaroori hai. Agar main bolun "Maine 2 computer kharide" — accounting kahegi: "Bhai, kitne rupaye ke hain?"

3
Recording (Likh Lo)

Jaise hi pata chala ki ye transaction paise wala hai, usse hum Journal mein likh lete hain — ye business ki "Diary" hai.

4
Classification (Bantwara)

Journal mein sab mix hota hai. Classification mein har cheez ka alag account banate hain — jise Ledger kehte hain. Salary ka alag, Cash ka alag, Purchase ka alag.

5
Summarising (Nishkarsh)

Saal ke end mein sab jod-ghata kar chota karte hain. Banti hain teen cheezein: Trial Balance → Profit & Loss A/c → Balance Sheet.

6
Analysis & Interpretation (Dimaag Lagao)

Profit hua toh kyun hua? Agle saal aur kaise badh sakta hai? Ye business ki "Report Card" check karne jaisa hai.

7
Communication (Batado)

Last mein ye saari information un logon ko di jaati hai jinka business mein interest hai — Malik, Bank, Tax wale.

Topic 2 — Accounting vs Book-Keeping

Ye dono alag hain, bhai!
📒
Book-Keeping

Ek routine kaam hai. Jaise rozana ki entry maarna. Ye junior staff karta hai. Isme dimaag kam, mehnat zyada lagti hai.

📊
Accounting

Wahan shuru hoti hai jahan Book-keeping khatam hoti hai. Isme analysis aur summary banti hai. Iske liye "Specialized Knowledge" chahiye hoti hai.

Topic 3 — Basic Accounting Terms (The Alphabet of Commerce)

Bhai, ye terms tere liye utne hi zaroori hain jitne PUBG mein 'Ammo'. Inhe dhyan se samajh:
1
Entity (Pehchan)

Accounts kehta hai "Business alag hai aur Malik alag." Agar malik business se apne ghar ke liye fridge kharidta hai — wo business ka asset nahi, malik ka personal kharcha (Drawings) mana jaayega.

2
Assets (Paisa Banane wali Machine)

Non-Current Assets: Jo lambe time (1 saal se zyada) tak saath nibhayenge — Machinery, Building, Furniture.
Current Assets: Jo ek saal ke andar cash mein badal jayenge — Stock, Debtors, Cash.
Tangible: Jinhe haath laga sako (Machinery). Intangible: Jinhe sirf mehsoos kar sako (Goodwill — business ki izzat/brand value).

3
Liabilities (Sir-dard/Udhaar)

Wo paisa jo business ko bahar walon ko dena hai.
Long-term: Bank se liya hua 5 saal wala loan.
Short-term: Creditors — jinse udhaar maal liya aur 2 mahine mein paise dene hain.

4
Capital

Wo "Seed Money" jo malik ne lagayi. Capital = Assets − Liabilities.

5
Debtors vs Creditors

Debtors (Asset): Wo log jinhe humne udhaar maal becha — inse paisa "Aayega".
Creditors (Liability): Wo log jinse humne udhaar liya — inhe paisa "Dena hai".

6
Revenue & Expenses

Revenue: Maal bech kar jo total paisa haath mein aaya.
Expenses: Wo kharche jo revenue kamane ke liye kiye gaye — Rent, Electricity, Salary.

Topic 4 — Users of Accounting Info (Kaun Kaun Nazar Rakhta Hai?)

🏠
Internal Users

Malik (taaki dekh sake kitna ameer hua) aur Management (taaki naye decisions le sake).

🌍
External Users

Banks: Loan dene se pehle dekhte hain ki tum chuka paoge ya nahi.
Investors: Paisa lagane se pehle check karte hain ki business dubega toh nahi.
Government: Tax calculate karne ke liye.
Employees: Bonus mangne ke liye ki "Bhai, profit hua hai toh humein bhi do!"

Topic 5 — Qualitative Characteristics (Quality Check)

1
Reliability

Information "Sachi" honi chahiye — jise proof kiya ja sake (Bill/Voucher ke saath).

2
Relevance

Sirf wahi likho jo kaam ka ho. Faltu baatein nahi.

3
Understandability

Aisa likho ki kisi ko bhi samajh aa jaye — chahe wo accountant na ho.

4
Comparability

Aisa system rakho ki is saal ke profit ko pichle saal se compare kar sakein.

Topic 6 — Business Transactions ka "Action-Reaction"

Bhai, business mein kuch bhi hota hai toh uska impact do jagah padta hai.

Economic Event: Jab bhi koi transaction hota hai, business ki "Financial Position" badal jaati hai — ya toh cash kam hoga, ya asset badhega, ya koi udhaar chadh jaayega.

Quantitative Change: Accounts sirf un cheezon ko bhaav deta hai jinhe hum numbers (Money) mein likh sakein.

Topic 7 — Role of Accounting (Sirf Hisaab Nahi, Business ki Jaan)

1
Language of Business

Jaise hum dukh-dard share karte hain — business apne profits aur losses "Financial Statements" ke zariye batata hai.

2
Historical Record

Ye business ki "Kundli" hai. 10 saal pehle kya kharida tha — sab yahan mil jaayega.

3
Information System

Ye ek aisi machine hai jisme 'Data' (bills) dalo aur 'Information' (reports) nikalo.

Topic 8 — Systems of Accounting (Kise Choose Karein?)

⭐
Double Entry System (The Hero) 🦸

Ye poori duniya mein chalta hai. Simple logic: "Every Debit has a Credit."
₹10,000 ka phone kharida? Phone (Asset) aaya → Cash (Asset) gaya. Balance sheet hamesha match hoti hai. Galti pakdi jaati hai.

🏠
Single Entry System (The Local Way)

Ise "Incomplete Records" bhi kehte hain. Jaise mohalle ke kirana store wale chacha karte hain — sirf cash aur debtors likhte hain. Profit sahi nahi nikalta. Bade businesses ke liye legal nahi hai.

Topic 9 — Basic Terms ka "Part 2" (More Keywords)

1
Purchases

Sirf tab use karo jab hum wo maal khareedein jise "Bechna" hai. Agar business ke liye AC kharida — wo 'Asset' hai, 'Purchase' nahi!

2
Purchase Returns & Sales Returns

Purchase Returns (Return Outward): Kharida hua maal wapas bhej diya — maal bahar gaya.
Sales Returns (Return Inward): Becha hua maal customer ne wapas kar diya — maal andar aaya.

3
Vouchers

Ye sabse bada proof hai. Kisi bhi entry se pehle ek paper chahiye (Bill, Receipt) — usse hi 'Voucher' kehte hain. Bina voucher ke accountant pen bhi nahi uthata.

4
Discount (Lalach ka khel)

Trade Discount: Bulk kharidne par milta hai. Accounts mein nahi dikhate — seedha value kam kar dete hain.
Cash Discount: Jaldi payment karne par milta hai. Accounts mein "Discount Allowed" ya "Discount Received" bolkar likhte hain.

5
Drawings

Jab malik business ki gallay se paise nikal kar apni biwi ke liye haar kharide ya bachche ki school fees bhar de — Capital kam ho jaati hai.

6
Bad Debts

Wo "Dost" jo paise lekar bhaag gaya! Jab Debtor se paisa milne ki umeed khatam ho jaaye — wo 'Bad Debt' (nuksan) ban jaata hai.

Topic 10 — Profit, Gain aur Loss (Farq Samjho!)

📈
Profit

Rozana ke business se bachta hai — Total Revenue − Total Expenses.

🎯
Gain

"Jackpot" jaisa — roz nahi hota. Business ki purani zameen mehnge mein bik gayi — wo 'Gain' hai.

📉
Loss

Jab kharche kamayi se zyada ho jayein. Ya achanak aag lag jaaye ya chori ho jaaye.

Topic 11 — Expenditure vs Expense

1
Capital Expenditure

Asset kharida (Laptop) — benefit 1 saal se zyada milega.

2
Revenue Expenditure

Roz ke kharche (Salary, Rent) — is saal use ho jaate hain.

Topic 12 — Stocks, Trade Receivable & Trade Payable

📦
Stocks / Inventory

Jo maal bika nahi — raw material (kacha maal), semi-finished (adha bana), aur finished goods (poora taiyaar) sab aata hai.

💸
Trade Receivable vs Trade Payable

Trade Receivable: Debtors + Bills Receivable — market se aane wala total paisa.
Trade Payable: Creditors + Bills Payable — jo humein chukana hai.

📋 Question & Answer — Exam Ready

Q1
Define Accounting.

Accounting is a systematic process of identifying, measuring, recording, classifying, summarising, interpreting, and communicating financial information to users for better decision-making.

Q2
State the end products of the accounting process.

The end products are Financial Statements: (1) Profit and Loss Account — shows net result of business operations. (2) Balance Sheet — shows the financial position of the business.

Q3
Who are the external users of accounting information?

External users include investors, lenders (banks), creditors, government/tax authorities, and the general public.

Q4
Enumerate the informational needs of management.

(1) Planning and forecasting future activities. (2) Decision-making on pricing, investments, and cost control. (3) Evaluating the performance of various departments.

Q5
Give three examples of revenues.

(1) Sales of goods or services. (2) Interest received on investments. (3) Commission or Rent received.

Q6
Distinguish between Debtors and Creditors.

Debtors: Persons who owe money to the business — purchased goods on credit. They are Assets.
Creditors: Persons to whom the business owes money — goods purchased on credit from them. They are Liabilities.

Q7
Explain the qualitative characteristics of accounting information.

(1) Reliability — factual and verifiable with source documents. (2) Relevance — influences decisions of users. (3) Understandability — simple and logical presentation. (4) Comparability — allows comparison across years and firms.

Q8
Describe the role of accounting in the modern world.

(1) Language of Business — communicates financial status to stakeholders. (2) Information System — processes raw data into meaningful reports. (3) Legal Requirement — mandatory for tax compliance and audits. (4) Decision Support — provides base data for complex financial decisions.

🏁 Key Takeaway

Accounting sirf entries yaad karna nahi hai — har transaction do jagah kyun affect karta hai — ye logic samajh lo, baaki sab easy ho jaata hai. Debit Credit — ek baar samjha toh life time kaam aayega.
